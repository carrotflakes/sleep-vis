Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 21, 2026

1. Overview

Sleep Vis ("the Service") is a free, open-source, client-side web application operated by carrotflakes. This Privacy Policy explains how the Service handles your information.

2. Data We Access

When you sign in with your Google account, the Service requests read-only access to your sleep data via the Google Health API (https://www.googleapis.com/auth/googlehealth.sleep.readonly).

The sleep data accessed includes:

  • Sleep session start and end times
  • Sleep stages (awake, light, deep, REM, etc.) with timestamps
  • Summary statistics (minutes asleep, minutes awake)
  • Data source information

3. How Your Data Is Processed

AspectDetail
Where data is processed Entirely in your browser. Your sleep data is fetched directly from Google's servers and rendered locally.
Server-side processing None. The Service has no backend server. No data is transmitted to the operator or any third party.
Data storage None. Data is held only in browser memory (React state) during your session. It is discarded when you close or refresh the page. No cookies, localStorage, or databases are used.
Data sharing None. Your data is never shared with, sold to, or disclosed to any third party.

4. Third-Party Services

The Service relies on the following third-party services:

  • Google Identity Services — for authentication (OAuth 2.0). Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Health API — for fetching your sleep data. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

No analytics, advertising, or tracking services are used.

5. Google API Services User Data Policy

Sleep Vis's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

6. Authentication & Access Tokens

The Service uses OAuth 2.0 access tokens to communicate with Google APIs. Tokens are held only in browser memory and are not stored persistently. You can revoke access at any time by:

  • Clicking "Sign Out" within the Service, or
  • Visiting your Google Account permissions page.

7. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. Continued use of the Service after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

9. Contact

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue at GitHub Issues.
